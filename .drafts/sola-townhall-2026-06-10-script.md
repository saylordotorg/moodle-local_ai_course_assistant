# SOLA Faculty Town Hall Script, 10 June 2026

Ten minutes total. Word for word. Slide cues in brackets. The live demo runs on production, learn.saylor.org, which is v5.4.5. Slides 4 and 5 are the demo backup. Timings assume a comfortable speaking pace of about 135 words per minute.

---

[SLIDE 1: Title. Stay here while people settle. Begin.]

[SLIDE 2: Every Saylor learner studies alone. Until now.] (0:00 to 1:30)

Good afternoon everyone. I want to show you something we have been building for our learners, and I want to start with the problem, because the problem is the whole reason this exists.

Saylor University runs on a model that most universities do not. Our courses are self paced. They are free. And we have no traditional teaching faculty. You all know this better than anyone, because you are the people who build the courses. But think about what that means from the learner's side. When a learner hits a wall in Unit 2 of BUS101 at two in the morning, there is no instructor. There are no office hours. There is no classmate in the next seat. For twenty years the choice at that moment has been: push through alone, or quietly give up. A lot of them give up.

SOLA, the Saylor Online Learning Assistant, changes that moment. It gives every learner, in every course, a personal tutor that is available around the clock, that teaches with questions instead of handing over answers, that speaks the learner's own language, and that is grounded in the exact course content you wrote. Not a generic chatbot. A tutor for your course.

[SLIDE 3: A tab on the edge of every course page] (1:30 to 2:15)

Here is what it looks like. There is no new app and no new login. SOLA lives inside the course page on learn.saylor.org, as a small tab on the right edge of the screen. You can see it here on the edge of this demo course. One click opens a chat drawer. And critically, SOLA knows where the learner is. It knows the course, it knows the page, it knows the unit. So when a learner says explain this page, it actually explains that page.

[SLIDE 4: First open, conversation starters] (2:15 to 3:00)

When a learner opens it for the first time, they do not get an empty box and a blinking cursor. They get conversation starters. Explain this page. Quiz me. Build a study plan. This matters because the learners who most need help are often the ones who do not know what to ask. The starters get them moving with one click.

[SLIDE 5: It coaches. It does not hand over answers.] (3:00 to 4:15)

Now the part I most want you to see, which is how it teaches. Look at this real exchange. The learner asks why a price ceiling causes shortages. Notice what SOLA does not do. It does not dump a definition. It asks the learner a question back: when the price of something drops, what happens to the number of people who want to buy it? The learner reasons it out loud, and gets it mostly right. Then SOLA confirms the reasoning, lays out the four step mechanism, and points the learner to Unit 2.3 of the course, by name, because it retrieved that section from the actual course materials. That is the Socratic pattern, and it is the default behavior, not a special mode.

And before anyone asks the question that every educator asks: yes, it refuses to do homework. If a learner pastes in a graded quiz question, SOLA declines and redirects them to the underlying concept. We test that behavior with an automated suite before every single release.

[SLIDE 6: 46 languages] (4:15 to 5:00)

One more thing about our learners. Most of them are outside the United States. SOLA detects the learner's language automatically and supports forty six of them. A learner can ask their question in Swahili or Hindi or Vietnamese and get coached through your course content in that language. They can also pin a preferred language in the settings panel you see here. Same tutor quality, regardless of first language. For a university with our global reach, this is an equity feature as much as a convenience feature.

[SLIDE 7: Built with guardrails] (5:00 to 5:45)

A quick word on guardrails, because trust is everything here. Academic integrity: as I said, it will not supply graded answers. Privacy: every learner sees a plain language consent notice before first use, and nothing is shared without it. Cost: there are spending caps per course and site wide, with alerts to administrators, so usage can never run away from us. And it stays on topic. Learners who try to use it as a general purpose chatbot get gently steered back to the course.

[SLIDE 8: Your course, measured] (5:45 to 6:30)

So what does SOLA give you, the people who build the courses? Two things. The first is visibility. This is the per course dashboard: conversations, active learners, messages per learner, usage trends over time. There is no grading burden here, no inbox to manage, nothing to moderate. You simply get to see, for the first time, how learners actually engage with the course you built after they leave the page view statistics behind.

[SLIDE 9: The questions learners are really asking] (6:30 to 7:30)

The second thing is, honestly, the one I would have wanted most when I was building courses. SOLA aggregates the question patterns learners ask, ranked by frequency. Look at this table. What is a real world example of, twenty times. I got a poor grade on, eleven times. I am struggling to stay focused, nine times. This is course revision gold. If learners in your course keep asking for a real world example of the same concept, that unit needs a better example. If they keep saying they are lost in week three, week three needs a checkpoint. You no longer have to guess where the course is confusing. The learners are telling you, every day, in their own words.

[SLIDE 10: You are in control] (7:30 to 8:00)

And you stay in control. SOLA is configured per course. You can enable it or disable it for your course, tune the welcome message, and it automatically hides itself on graded quizzes. The learning objectives you define become the spine it coaches toward. Nothing changes in your course unless you choose it.

[SLIDE 11: Live demo] (8:00 to 9:30)

Let me show you the real thing, live on production.

[Open learn.saylor.org, BUS101, logged in as the demo learner account. Narrate while clicking.]

Here is BUS101 as a learner sees it. There is the SOLA tab on the right edge. I click it, and here is the drawer with the conversation starters we saw. Now I will ask it the same question from the slide. Why does a price ceiling cause shortages?

[Wait for the streamed response. While it streams:] Notice it is streaming in real time, and notice the shape of the answer: it is going to coach, not lecture.

[When the response lands:] And there it is, a guiding question back to me, grounded in this course.

One more. I will ask the same thing in Spanish. Por que un tope de precios causa escasez?

[Wait for response.] Same tutor, same course grounding, now in Spanish, automatically.

[FALLBACK if the demo misbehaves, say exactly this:] The wifi gods have opinions today, so let me show you exactly what that looks like. [Return to slides 4 and 5 and walk the screenshots.]

[SLIDE 12: Where we are, and how to get involved] (9:30 to 10:00)

So, where we are. SOLA is live today on Learn and Degrees in a pilot of about thirty courses. Nearly three thousand learners used it during the pilot window. The cost at full pilot scale is a few hundred dollars a month, with hard caps, which for a tutor that serves every learner simultaneously is a remarkable number.

What we need from you is three things. Tell us which of your courses should join the rollout next. Send us the confusing spots you already know about, and we will check them against what learners are asking. And give us fifteen minutes to set you up with the analytics dashboard for your course. I will be around after this meeting, and my inbox is always open.

Every Saylor learner studies alone. They do not have to anymore. Thank you.

## Presenter notes

- Total spoken length is about 1,380 words, which is right at ten minutes with the demo pauses.
- The demo is on production, v5.4.5. Everything shown in the demo and the slides exists in v5.4.5. Do not show or mention: the learning path map, the readiness nudge, premium routing, or any voice or avatar features. Voice and avatars are future direction only and are not in this presentation at all.
- Pre demo checklist, do this in the thirty minutes before the meeting:
  1. Log in to learn.saylor.org as the demo learner in a clean browser profile.
  2. Open BUS101 once, accept the consent notice so it does not appear on stage.
  3. Ask one throwaway question to warm the provider connection, then clear or reset.
  4. Zoom the browser to 125 percent so the back row can read the drawer.
  5. Keep this deck open behind the browser. Slides 4 and 5 are the backup; the transition line is in the script.
- The seeded numbers on slides 8 and 9 are demo data from our development environment, and the footer on those slides says so. If asked, say exactly that: the dashboard screenshots use seeded demo data, the pilot numbers on the last slide are real.
- Likely questions and one line answers:
  - Does it help learners cheat? No. It refuses graded questions and redirects to the concept, and we regression test that on every release.
  - What does it cost? A few hundred dollars per month at pilot scale with hard caps; scaling estimates are budgeted and reviewed by finance.
  - What about learner privacy? Consent before first use, no training of vendor models on our data, GDPR aligned, and learners can self serve delete their conversation history.
  - Can I turn it off for my course? Yes, per course, one setting.
  - Which AI does it use? A mix of commercial models chosen per task by benchmark; the routing is ours and we can swap vendors without learners noticing.

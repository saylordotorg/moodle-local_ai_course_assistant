## Slide 2 — What SOLA is (1.5 min)

**On screen:**
> ## A coach on every course page
> - Opens from a tab on the side of the page
> - Answers questions, quizzes the learner, points them to what's next
> - Always on, patient, in dozens of languages
> - The learner never has to leave the page they're studying

**Speaker notes:**
"SOLA is a chat coach on the edge of every course page. A learner clicks a tab, a panel slides open, and they can ask anything about what they're studying, right there, without leaving the page.

But it's more than a question box. It can quiz a learner on the section they're reading and show them how they did, and it can tell them what to focus on next — any time of day, patiently, in dozens of languages. Think of it as a teaching assistant available to every learner, at two in the morning, in their own language. Now let me show you why it's different from a generic chatbot, because that difference is where you come in."

---

## Slide 3 — It reads *your* course (the key idea) (1.5 min)

**On screen:**
> ## Not a generic chatbot — it knows the course
> - SOLA reads the course content and the page the learner is on
> - Its answers are about *your* material, not the open internet
> - Which means: the better the course, the better the tutor

**Speaker notes:**
"Here's the single most important idea. SOLA is not ChatGPT in a Saylor wrapper. Before it answers, it reads the content of the specific course, and the specific page, the learner is on. Its answers are grounded in your material.

So if a learner on a page about supply and demand asks for another example, SOLA pulls from that course's treatment, in that course's framing — not some random corner of the internet. And that has a direct consequence for this room: because SOLA's help is built on your course, the quality of the tutor rises and falls with the quality of the course design. SOLA doesn't replace good course design — it rewards it. That's the theme I'll keep coming back to."

## Slide 4 — What learners get (1.5 min)

**On screen:**
> ## For the learner
> - Grounded answers about this course, as they read
> - Practice quizzes, on demand, with feedback
> - "What should I focus on?" — pointed at their weakest spot
> - "Where does this lead?" — their path through a program
> - Works in 46 languages, available 24/7

**Speaker notes:**
"Quickly, the learner's side. First, grounded help as they read — the part we just covered. Second, practice: a learner can say 'quiz me on this,' and SOLA generates questions, checks the answers, and tells them how they did. Third, direction: 'what should I focus on' points them at the specific objective they're weakest on, instead of 'just review the chapter.' And fourth, the bigger picture — if a course is part of a degree or certificate, SOLA can show the learner their whole path and nudge them on when they're ready. All of it works in dozens of languages, around the clock, which matters enormously for a global, self-paced university."

## Slide 5 — What this means for course design (2 min)  ★ the ask-behind-the-ask

**On screen:**
> ## Your design powers the tutor
> | If your course has… | SOLA can… |
> |---|---|
> | Clear learning objectives | Track mastery + tell learners what to focus on |
> | Well-structured content | Ground answers accurately |
> | A coherent program/path | Show learners where the course leads |
> Good course design = a smarter tutor

**Speaker notes:**
"This slide is really why I asked for time today. Everything SOLA does well traces back to a course-design choice you make.

Take objectives. SOLA's mastery tracking, the 'what should I focus on' feature, the readiness nudges — all of that runs on objectives. Clear objectives give learners a tutor that can coach them toward specific goals; vague or missing ones give them a tutor that can only answer questions.

Take content structure. SOLA grounds its answers by searching your material — clean, well-organized content means accurate, on-target answers. And programs: the path features only light up when courses are arranged into coherent degrees and certificates.

So the headline is simple: the work you already do to make a course clear and well-structured is exactly the work that makes SOLA shine. You're not being asked to design for the AI — you're being asked to keep designing well, and SOLA turns that into a better experience for every learner."

## Slide 6 — Boundaries and trust (1.5 min)

**On screen:**
> ## Safe, private, and under control
> - Does **not** replace you, and **not** grade or change grades
> - Stays on coursework — guardrailed against going off-topic
> - **Advisory, off by default, controlled per course**
> - Clear consent notice; we log activity, never conversation content
> - Full data-erasure on request (GDPR); cost-capped; instant off switch

**Speaker notes:**
"Let me be just as clear about boundaries, because that matters for trust. SOLA does not replace you — it teaches from the course you wrote; it doesn't author it or touch grades. It's built to stay on coursework, with a guardrail against being talked off the rails. And it's conservative by default: the teaching behaviors are off until someone turns them on, per course, before anything goes site-wide.

On privacy: the first time a learner opens SOLA they get a plain-language notice they have to read before accepting. We log that a conversation happened, never its content. If a learner asks to be forgotten, we can erase everything we hold. And operationally it's cost-capped, hardened against misuse, with a single switch to take it offline instantly. Built to be trustworthy, not just clever."

## Optional appendix / backup slides (if questions go deep)
- **"How does it stay on my course?"** — it reads the course content and current page, allocates most of its attention to that material, and is honest when it can't find something rather than making it up.
- **"What does it cost?"** — usage-based; there are spend caps and a kill switch, and we benchmark providers on quality-vs-cost.
- **"Languages?"** — the interface works in dozens of languages, auto-detected.
- **"Can I turn off a feature I don't want?"** — yes, everything is per-course and default-off.
- **"What's on the roadmap?"** (only if asked) — spoken voice interaction and on-screen avatars are directions we're exploring for the future; they are **not** part of the current rollout, which is text-based. Don't promise a date.
